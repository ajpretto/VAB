# Cold Call Script — AI Voice Assistant for Home Services

**Goal:** Book a 15-minute demo call
**Mindset:** You're not selling software. You're offering to solve their #1 headache.

---

## OPENER (10 seconds)

> "Hey [Name], this is Anthony. I help [plumbers/HVAC guys/etc] in Tampa stop losing jobs to missed calls. Got 30 seconds?"

**If they say no / bad time:**
> "No problem — when's better, tomorrow morning or afternoon?"

**If they're skeptical:**
> "I'll be quick — just wanted to see if this is even relevant to you."

---

## HOOK (20 seconds)

> "I set up AI phone systems that answer your calls 24/7 — books appointments, qualifies emergencies, texts you a summary. Works while you're on a job site. Full white-glove setup, you don't touch anything technical."

**Pause. Let them react.**

---

## PAIN QUESTION (the key moment)

> "Quick question — what happens right now when someone calls and you're under a sink or up on a roof?"

**LISTEN. Let them talk.**

Common responses:
- "Goes to voicemail" → "And how many of those actually call back?"
- "My wife/office handles it" → "What about nights and weekends?"
- "I try to call back" → "How long does that usually take?"
- "I lose some" → "Any idea how many per week?"

---

## AGITATE (make the pain real)

> "Yeah, I hear that a lot. The math is brutal — if you're missing even 5 calls a week at $500 per job, that's $10K a month walking out the door."

**Pause.**

> "Most of those people aren't leaving voicemails. They're calling the next guy on Google."

---

## SOLUTION (brief)

> "So what I do is set up an AI receptionist that:
> - Answers every call instantly, 24/7
> - Books appointments into your calendar
> - Texts you a summary after each call
> - Handles nights, weekends, holidays
> 
> I handle all the setup. Takes about a week. You don't have to do anything technical."

---

## QUALIFY THEM

> "Can I ask — how many calls do you typically get per day?"

**Listen for:**
- Volume (more calls = more value)
- Current solution (or lack of one)
- Pain level (frustrated = good prospect)

> "And who handles calls now? Is that working well?"

---

## PRICING CONVERSATION

### When they ask "How much?"

> "It's $1,997 a month with a $1,500 setup fee. That covers the custom build, integrations, and ongoing support."

**Pause. Let them respond.**

**If they react to price:**

> "Yeah, it's an investment. But here's how I look at it — if you're losing even 5 calls a week at $500 each, that's $10K a month. This pays for itself in the first week."

> "Plus, a part-time receptionist would cost you $2-3K a month, and they can't work 24/7 or handle 10 calls at once during a storm."

### Founding Partner Offer (use strategically)

> "Actually — I'm taking on a few founding partners right now. Discounted setup and monthly rate in exchange for a testimonial and case study. Would that be interesting to you?"

**Only offer if:**
- They're clearly interested but price-hesitant
- They seem like they'd be a good case study
- You haven't filled 5 slots yet

**Founding Partner pricing:**
- $500 setup (vs $1,500)
- $997/month (vs $1,997)

---

## TRIAL CLOSE

> "Here's what I'd like to do — let me set this up and you try it for 2 weeks. If it doesn't catch at least a couple jobs you would've missed, I'll refund the setup fee. Fair?"

**Note:** Lead with full price. Only offer trial/guarantee if they need it to close.

---

## HANDLE OBJECTIONS

### "That's expensive"
> "I hear you. Let me ask — what's a typical job worth to you? [$500-800 usually]
> 
> So if this catches just 4 jobs a month that would've gone to voicemail, it's paid for itself. Most of my clients see that in the first week."

### "I already have someone answering phones"
> "That's great — what happens when they're on another call? Or after 5pm? This handles the overflow and the nights/weekends. It's not replacing anyone, it's backing them up."

### "I don't trust AI / Customers won't like it"
> "Fair concern. Here's the thing — most people can't tell it's AI. It sounds like a real receptionist. And if someone asks, it's honest about it.
>
> The real question is: would you rather have AI answer, or have it go to voicemail? Because that's the choice at 9pm."

### "I'm too busy right now"
> "That's actually the whole point — you're busy ON jobs, so you're missing calls FOR new jobs. This runs in the background while you work.
>
> When's a better week to set up a quick call?"

### "Send me some info"
> "Sure, what's your email? Actually — let me text you a link right now. What's your cell?"

*(Get their cell = direct line for follow-up)*

### "I need to think about it"
> "Totally fair. What specifically are you thinking through? Maybe I can answer it now."

### "Let me talk to my wife/partner"
> "Makes sense. What do you think their main concern would be? ... 
>
> How about I call you back [tomorrow/Thursday] after you've had a chance to chat?"

---

## BOOK THE DEMO

> "Let's do a quick 15-minute call where I show you exactly how it sounds and works. I can do [tomorrow at 10] or [Thursday afternoon]. What's better?"

**Always give 2 options, not "when works for you?"**

---

## CLOSE THE CALL

> "Got you down for [day/time]. I'll send a calendar invite. Looking forward to it, [Name]."

---

## AFTER THE CALL

Send to Gru via voice note:
> "[Company name], [Name], [outcome], [next steps], [notes]"

Example:
> "ABC Plumbing, talked to Mike the owner, interested, demo Thursday 2pm. Missing about 10 calls a week, wife handles phones but not after 5pm. Good candidate for founding partner."

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **Talk less, listen more** — Ask questions, let them sell themselves on the problem
2. **Lead with full price** — Don't discount immediately
3. **Use their language** — If they say "leads" use "leads", if they say "calls" use "calls"  
4. **Assume the sale** — "When should we do the demo?" not "Would you like a demo?"
5. **Get the cell phone** — Email goes to spam. Text doesn't.
6. **Founding Partner is a tool** — Use it to close, not as your default offer

---

## QUICK REFERENCE

**Your one-liner:**
> "I help [trade] businesses stop losing jobs to missed calls using AI."

**The math:**
> "5 missed calls/week × $500/job = $10,000/month lost"

**The positioning:**
> "$1,997/month is less than a part-time receptionist, and it works 24/7."

**The close:**
> "Let me set it up — if it doesn't pay for itself in week one, I'll refund the setup."
