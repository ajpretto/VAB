# Cold Call Script — AI Voice Assistant for Real Estate Agents

**Goal:** Book a 15-minute demo call  
**Mindset:** You're solving their #1 problem — missed leads when they can't answer

---

## OPENER (10 seconds)

> "Hey [Name], this is Anthony. Quick question — what happens to your leads when you're in a showing and can't answer the phone?"

**Let them answer.** They'll describe the pain.

**If they say "bad time":**
> "No problem — when's better, tomorrow morning or afternoon?"

**If they're skeptical:**
> "I'll be quick — just checking if this is even relevant to you."

---

## HOOK (20 seconds)

> "Got it. So here's why I called — I set up AI phone systems specifically for real estate agents. It answers your calls 24/7, qualifies the lead — are they buying, selling, pre-approved, what's their timeline — and texts you a summary before you even leave the showing. Full white-glove setup, you don't touch anything technical."

**Pause. Let them react.**

---

## GATEKEEPER (if you don't reach the agent)

Real estate agents usually answer their own phone, but if you get an assistant:

> "Hey, is [Agent Name] available? I help agents stop losing leads to missed calls."

**If asked what it's about:**
> "I work with real estate agents on AI phone systems. When's the best time to catch them?"

**Get their cell:** Agents often have a direct line — ask for it.

---

## PAIN QUESTION (the key moment)

> "Let me ask — roughly how many calls do you think go to voicemail when you're showing houses or in meetings?"

**LISTEN. Let them talk.**

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What happens to those callers who don't leave a voicemail?"
- "How often do you hear 'I called someone else' when you follow up?"
- "What about evenings and weekends — that's when buyers are browsing Zillow, right?"

---

## AGITATE (make the pain real)

> "Yeah, here's the brutal part — 85% of callers don't leave voicemail. They just call the next agent on Zillow. And 78% of buyers work with whoever responds first."

**Pause.**

> "So if you're missing even 3-4 qualified leads a month and one would've closed... that's a $12,000 commission that just walked."

---

## SOLUTION (brief)

> "So what I do is set up an AI receptionist that:
> - Answers every call instantly, day or night
> - Asks if they're buying, selling, or inquiring about a listing
> - Qualifies them — timeline, budget, pre-approved?
> - Books showings right into your calendar
> - Texts you a summary immediately
> 
> You get a qualified lead with full context before you even call them back. And they feel like they talked to someone — not voicemail."

---

## QUALIFY THEM

> "Quick question — are you a solo agent or part of a team?"

**Listen for:**
- Solo = simpler sale, $497-797/mo tier
- Team = bigger opportunity, $1,497/mo tier

> "And what CRM do you use? Follow Up Boss? kvCORE?"

*This shows expertise and sets up integration conversation.*

---

## PRICING CONVERSATION

### When they ask "How much?"

> "Most solo agents are at $497 a month. That includes unlimited calls, full setup, and I handle everything technical."

**Pause. Let them respond.**

**If they react to price:**

> "I hear you. But here's how I think about it — one saved commission is $12K+. This pays for itself probably the first month. And it's way cheaper than hiring someone part-time."

### Compare to alternatives:

> "A human answering service is $200-400 a month and they can't qualify leads or book appointments. A virtual assistant is $1,500+. This is better than both at half the price."

---

## HANDLE OBJECTIONS

### "My clients want to talk to ME"

> "Totally. That's actually the point — the AI qualifies them and books the appointment, then YOU call them back personally within minutes. You're still the relationship. It's just a 24/7 safety net so no one slips through."

### "I already have a system"

> "What happens at 8pm on Saturday when someone wants to see a house Sunday morning? Or when you're mid-showing and a hot lead calls?"

### "That seems expensive"

> "What's one lost commission worth? For most agents that's $10-15K. This is $497/month. If it saves you ONE deal a year, it's paid for itself 20x over."

### "I don't trust AI / My clients won't like it"

> "Fair concern. Here's the thing — it doesn't sound like a robot. It sounds like a professional assistant. And when you call them back 5 minutes later with their full info, they're impressed, not annoyed."

### "I'm too busy right now"

> "That's actually the point — you're busy with clients, which means you're missing new leads. This handles the calls you can't get to. When's a better week to set up a quick call?"

### "Send me some info"

> "Sure — actually, let me just text you a link right now. What's the best cell to reach you?"

*(Get their cell = direct line for follow-up)*

### "I need to think about it"

> "Makes sense. What specifically are you weighing? Maybe I can answer it now."

---

## BOOK THE DEMO

> "Let me show you exactly how it works — you'll call the number, pretend you're a buyer, and experience it yourself. Takes 15 minutes. I can do tomorrow at 10 or Thursday afternoon. What's better?"

**Always give 2 options.**

---

## CLOSE THE CALL

> "Got you down for [day/time]. I'll send a calendar invite. Looking forward to showing you how this works, [Name]."

---

Send to Gru via voice note:

> "Sarah Miller, Keller Williams, interested, demo Thursday 2pm. Solo agent, uses Follow Up Boss, says she misses calls during showings constantly. Good candidate."

>
> If that sounds useful, give me a call back at [your number] or I'll try you again soon. Talk soon."

**Keep it under 30 seconds.**

---

## TEXT FOLLOW-UP (after voicemail)

> "Hey [Name], Anthony here. Left you a voicemail about never missing a lead again. Quick q: what happens when buyers call while you're in a showing? I help Tampa agents fix that. Worth a quick chat?"

---

## DM SCRIPT (Instagram/LinkedIn)

> "Hey [Name]! Quick question — what happens to leads that call when you're mid-showing? I help agents set up AI that answers 24/7, qualifies buyers, and books appointments. Would you be open to a quick demo?"

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **Lead with the pain** — "What happens when you can't answer?" is more powerful than pitching features
2. **Use their language** — If they say "leads" use "leads", if they say "clients" use "clients"
3. **The math wins** — One commission = $12K. This is $497/month. Simple.
4. **Demo > Explanation** — Always push to show, not tell
5. **Get the cell phone** — Agents live on their cell, email goes to spam

---

**The math:**

**The close:**
