# Writing Conventions — Anthony's Outreach

## Voice & Tone
- First person, college student tone — casual but thoughtful
- Should read like a student who paid attention in class, not a press release
- Contractions are fine ("didn't", "wasn't", "he's")
- Address people as **Mr./Mrs./Ms. + last name** (never first names)
- **Sound like an eager student who's excited to learn and genuinely pursuing greatness**

## Things to Avoid (AI Tells)
- No significance inflation ("pivotal moment", "testament to", "reshaping the landscape")
- No promotional language ("renowned", "groundbreaking", "vibrant")
- No rule-of-three constructions
- No em dashes (—)
- No negative parallelisms ("It's not just X, it's Y")
- No "-ing" participial phrases tacked on for fake depth
- Don't substitute "is"/"are" with "serves as" or "stands as"
- **No generic questions** ("How do you choose your projects?", "What advice would you give?")

## Required Fields
- Always include the person's **email address** in the outreach card/message
- Subject lines always start with **"Anthony Pretto —"** followed by the hook

## Good Patterns
- Admit what you don't know ("I don't know much about...")
- Show you're still figuring things out ("I'm still figuring out which direction...")
- Be specific about what you want to learn
- Keep asks simple and direct ("Would you have 15 minutes for a coffee or a call?")
- End with just "Thanks," not "Thanks for considering," or other fluff
- **Reference specific things they've done, said, or written**
- **Show you've actually researched them, not just Googled their title**

## Pre-Outreach Research Workflow (MANDATORY)

Before writing ANY networking email, complete this research:

### 1. Deep Background (15-20 min per person)
- [ ] LinkedIn profile — full work history, posts, articles they've written
- [ ] Google News — recent press mentions, interviews, quotes
- [ ] Company bio page — official description
- [ ] Podcast appearances — listen for personality, stories, opinions
- [ ] Twitter/X — what do they post about? What's their vibe?
- [ ] Conference talks or panels — any YouTube videos?
- [ ] Published articles or interviews — direct quotes to reference

### 2. Find the Personal Hook
- [ ] Specific project they worked on that you can mention
- [ ] A quote or opinion they shared publicly
- [ ] A career move or decision that's interesting
- [ ] Something they're clearly passionate about
- [ ] Shared connection (school, city, organization, interest)

### 3. Draft the Email
- [ ] Open with something specific you found (not "I came across your work")
- [ ] Reference their actual words, projects, or decisions
- [ ] Ask a specific question based on your research (not generic)
- [ ] Show genuine curiosity about something they uniquely know

### 4. Quality Checklist (before adding to Mission Control)
- [ ] Does this email prove I researched them? (Would it work for anyone else? If yes, rewrite)
- [ ] Is there a personal hook in the first 2 sentences?
- [ ] Is my question specific to their experience?
- [ ] Does it sound eager and excited, not transactional?
- [ ] No AI tells (run through conventions above)
- [ ] Mr./Ms. + last name used
- [ ] Email address included

### 5. Save Research
- Save research notes to `memory/networking/research/[LASTNAME].md`
- Include sources, quotes, key facts for follow-up conversations

---

## Post-Draft Process
1. Run through humanizer to strip remaining AI patterns
2. Final manual audit checking for natural student voice, varied structure, and correct spelling
3. Verify against Quality Checklist above

---

*Apply these conventions to all networking emails, cold outreach, and professional correspondence.*
