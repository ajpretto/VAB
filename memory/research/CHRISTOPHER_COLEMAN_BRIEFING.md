## Talking Points If You Attend

### If you get a chance to introduce yourself:

**Script:**
> "Hi Mr. Coleman, I'm Anthony Pretto — I'm interning with Wingspan in Mount Prospect. I saw you were speaking today and wanted to come learn more about the suburban development landscape. The work on projects like 20 West and the Skokie development has been really interesting to see from the inside."

**Why this works:**
- Identifies you as an insider (Wingspan intern)
- Shows initiative (came to see him speak)
- References specific projects (proves you've done homework)
- Doesn't ask for anything (relationship first)

### Questions you could ask (if there's Q&A):

**Safe/smart questions:**
1. "What do you look for when evaluating whether a suburban site is worth developing that others might overlook?"
2. "How has the dynamic between developers and municipalities evolved in the northwest suburbs?"
3. "With the vertical integration across the Nicholas Family of Companies, how do you decide when to use internal capabilities vs. outside partners?"

**Avoid:**
- Anything that sounds like you're angling for a job
- Anything about internal Wingspan business (don't put him on the spot)
- Generic questions with obvious answers

### If you can't attend but want to mention it at work:

**To your manager:**
> "I saw that Christopher Coleman is speaking at the Bisnow event tomorrow about suburban development. Is there any way for me to attend, or is that something that's more for senior people?"

**Why this works:**
- Shows you're paying attention to industry events
- Shows you know who the leadership is
- Gives them an easy out if it's not appropriate
- Plants the seed that you're engaged
