# Cuby Technologies — Research Brief

*Compiled: 2026-03-09*

---

## The Big Idea

**"The product is not the house — it's the factory."**

Cuby builds Mobile Micro-Factories (MMFs) — portable, deployable factories that produce "kits of parts" for homes. They're not building houses. They're building the machines that build houses.

---

## The Problem They're Solving

- Construction is the only major industry that hasn't industrialized
- 88% of contractors can't fill skilled labor positions (AGC survey)
- 40% of workforce retiring in next 10 years
- Productivity has lagged other sectors for decades

---

## How It Works

**Mobile Micro-Factory (MMF):**
- Produces complete kit-of-parts (foundation to finishes)
- Ships within 150-200 mile radius
- Step-by-step assembly instructions included
- **4 unskilled workers** can assemble home in **45-60 days**
- Toyota Production System — every step verified before next

**Economics:**
- MMF capex: ~$7.5M (plus land, infrastructure, working capital)
- Target build cost: $100-110/sqft
- ~20% below top "product" builders
- IBC compliant with local calibrations

**Anti-Gigafactory Strategy:**
- Small, distributed factories vs. one massive facility
- Faster to deploy
- Tied to committed offtake
- Right-sized to local demand
- Avoids Katerra/Veev failure mode ($1.5B+ raised, failed)

---

## Current Status (March 2026)

- R&D facility in Belarus (400,000+ engineering hours)
- First US MMF in **Pahrump, Nevada** — launching Q3/Q4 2026
- Tied to **3,300 single-family home pipeline** with local developer
- Actively partnering with more Nevada builders
- 10-year plan: ~275 micro factories, ~200,000 homes, ~300,000 jobs

## Why This Matters for Anthony

Cuby is automating the **physical construction** process. The opportunity is automating the **operational/back-office** side:
- Budget tracking
- Draw management
- Vendor coordination
- Project documentation

As Cuby scales and onboards more builder partners, operational complexity increases. Software/AI automation becomes more valuable.

**Angle:** "What operational pain points exist outside the physical build?"
