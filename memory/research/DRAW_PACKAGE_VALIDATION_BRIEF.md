## 📄 The G702/G703 Pain

AIA Forms G702 (Application for Payment) and G703 (Schedule of Values) are industry standard but universally hated:

> "Completing billing forms can be a time-consuming process."  
> — Trimble/Flashtract

> "Manually preparing G702 and G703 forms, tracking progress, and managing supporting documents can be time-consuming and prone to human error."  
> — Archdesk

> "Manual data entry leads to errors in scheduled values, stored materials, or retainage."  
> — Kolena

**The pattern:** Forms are necessary but tedious. Everyone tolerates them. No one likes them.

## 📋 Draw Package Components (What Gets Chased)

A complete draw package typically includes:
1. **G702/G703** — Application for payment + schedule of values
2. **Sub invoices** — From every active subcontractor
3. **Lien waivers** — Conditional and unconditional, from every sub
4. **Change order documentation** — If applicable
5. **Progress photos** — Proof work is complete
6. **Inspection reports** — Third-party verification
7. **Budget-to-actual** — Updated cost tracking

**The #1 cause of draw delays:** Incomplete documentation  
**The most tedious part:** Chasing lien waivers from subs who don't respond

## 💬 Questions to Ask GCs

### Opening (Establish Rapport)
- "How do you currently handle draw packages for your projects?"
- "Walk me through what a typical month-end billing cycle looks like."

### Pain Discovery
- "How long does it typically take to compile a draw package?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of the process?"
- "How often do you get pushback from lenders on documentation?"
- "How much time do you spend chasing lien waivers from subs?"

### Quantify the Pain
- "If you had to guess, how many hours per month does this take?"
- "Has a delayed draw ever caused you to miss payroll or delay a sub payment?"
- "Do you pad your bids to account for payment delays?"

### Solution Testing
- "If something could cut that time in half, what would it be worth?"
- "What would you need to see to trust a new tool?"
- "What have you tried before that didn't work?"

### Close
- "Who else at your company deals with this?"
- "Would you be open to a follow-up if I build something?"
