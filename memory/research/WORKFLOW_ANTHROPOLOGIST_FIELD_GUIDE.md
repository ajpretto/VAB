# 🔍 Workflow Anthropologist Field Guide

*Quick reference for Monday at Wingspan. Keep this open on your phone.*

---

## The Mission

**Week 1 Goal:** Find ONE process that would be 10x better with AI + automation.

**Today's Job:** Observe. Ask. Document. Don't solve yet.

---

## 👀 What to Watch For

### Red Flags (Pain = Opportunity)

| You See... | It Means... |
|------------|-------------|
| Someone copying data between spreadsheets | Manual data entry = automation target |
| "Let me check my email for that" | Information scattered = centralization opportunity |
| Someone on hold / chasing someone | Communication bottleneck = workflow gap |
| Printing documents to review | Paper-dependent process = digitization opportunity |
| "We always do it this way" | Legacy process = modernization target |
| Frustrated sighs | PAIN. Ask about it. |

### Magic Phrases to Listen For

- "This takes forever"
- "I have to do this every week/month"
- "I'm waiting on..."
- "We don't have a good way to..."
- "It's in an email somewhere"
- "I keep a spreadsheet for that"
- "Let me check with [person]"

---

## 🗣️ Questions to Ask (Casually)

### Warm-Up (Build Rapport First)
- "What's keeping you busy today?"
- "What's the most interesting project you're working on?"
- "How long have you been doing this?"

### Process Discovery
- "Walk me through how that works?"
- "What happens after that?"
- "Who else touches this?"
- "Where does this info come from / go to?"

### Pain Point Validation
- "What's the most tedious part of your day?"
- "Is there something that always takes longer than it should?"
- "What do you wish you didn't have to do manually?"
- "What falls through the cracks sometimes?"

### The Gold Question
> "If you had a magic assistant who could do any task perfectly, what would you have them do first?"

---

## 📝 How to Document

### In the Moment
Use your phone Notes app. Quick bullets:

```
OBSERVATION: [Person] spent 20 min updating spreadsheet
PROCESS: Draw tracking for [Project X]
PAIN: Manual copy-paste from emails
WHO: [Name], [Role]
```

### End of Day
Transfer to `memory/wingspan/2026-03-10.md`:
- What processes did you observe?
- Who did you talk to?
- What pain points surfaced?
- What surprised you?

## 🎯 High-Value Process Areas

Based on research, these are common pain points in RE development. Listen for them:

| Process | What to Ask |
|---------|-------------|
| **Budget Tracking** | "How do you track project costs vs budget?" |
| **Draw Management** | "How do you handle construction draw requests?" |
| **Vendor/Contractor** | "How do you track contractor performance?" |
| **Document Management** | "Where do RFIs and submittals get stored?" |
| **Forecasting** | "How do you project cash flow?" |
| **Reporting** | "What reports do you produce? How often?" |

---

## 🚫 Don't

- Don't pitch AI solutions (yet)
- Don't interrupt people who are busy
- Don't take notes in a way that makes people uncomfortable
- Don't ask "why don't you just...?" — observe first, solve later

---

## ✅ Do

- Be genuinely curious
- Ask follow-up questions
- Thank people for their time
- Look for patterns across conversations
- Notice what tools/software people use

---

You're not there to fix things. You're there to understand them.

The best automation opportunities come from deep understanding, not surface-level guesses.

Be a sponge. Absorb everything. Filter later.
