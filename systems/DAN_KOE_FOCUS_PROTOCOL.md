## The 1-Hour Deep Work Protocol

### Step 1: Vision & Anti-Vision (One-Time Setup)

- Become brutally aware of what you **DON'T want** — where you're headed if nothing changes
- Sit with that discomfort until it's unbearable
- Write your ideal future — the goals you shelved because they aren't "rational"
- If friends and family think you couldn't achieve them, **even better**
- Map everything: ideal future → exact first step
- List every distraction standing in your way
- Then **disappear** — not from life, but from what binds you to old ways
- Remove people, apps, activities that don't add to your well-being
- You don't need to explain yourself
- Frame: 10-year → 1-year → 1-month → 1-week goals

### Step 2: Hierarchy of Goals (One-Time Setup)

- Big goals for direction, small goals for clarity
- You're making an educated guess — adjust in real time
- Break down until tasks are **stupidly simple** — can't help but complete them
- Choose **ONE project** that moves the needle toward your vision
- Brain dump everything. Study 3-5 examples. Outline into milestones.

### Step 3: Project-Based Learning

- **Don't start by learning. Start with what you know.**
- Try → fail → search only when stuck → apply immediately
- Learning comes from struggle, not memorization
- How much you learn = how much progress you make on the project
- Every day: **1-3 priority tasks** that advance the project
- **That's the only productivity advice you need**
- Rule: After 2 weeks with no visible progress → wrong direction

### Step 4: Daily Lever-Moving Tasks (Your 1-Hour Commitment)

**Morning:** Productivity block on #1 priority
**Midday:** Creativity block (walk, think, rest)
**Repeat as needed, then hard stop** — let your mind work subconsciously

## Embracing Uncertainty

> Uncertainty is signal, not noise.

- You're **supposed** to feel lost, overwhelmed, like you have no idea what you're doing
- What did you expect when you decided to change your life?
- Successful people don't perceive uncertainty as dangerous — they act anyway
- All outsized gains lie in your ability to **embrace, manage, and extend uncertainty**
- The "certain" life (job, 401k) is the **least rewarding**
- You must invest in your **portfolio of failures** until you can afford to succeed
